INSPIRE Data Specification Extensions

Get the most out of INSPIRE by extending data specifications!

The Study

In this project, we collect and document best practices for extending INSPIRE models. The resulting guidance will benefit organisations that want to use INSPIRE as a reference infrastructure. The documentation is centered on a pattern catalogue that describes common extension approaches and explains their impact on implementation. Through surveys and workshops, the INSPIRE community will be involved from the start on.

Content of the Study

The study has three core elements: The initial survey and inventory of existing INSPIRE Model Extensions, the extension pattern catalogue and an end-to-end tutorial project. The sections will go online one by one over the next months:

  1. Introduction
  2. Results of the Survey (ETA: April 15th)
  3. Model Extensions Inventory (ETA: April 20th)
  4. INSPIRE Methodology summary (ETA: April 30th)
  5. Model Extension Methodology (ETA: April 30th)
  6. The Pattern Catalogue (ETA: May 30th)
  7. An End-to-End Tutorial Project (ETA: May 30th)
  8. Summary and Conclusions (ETA: July 20th)

Contact and Involvement

Interested in learning more or in getting involved? Send us a note to be registered as an observer or to take part in the advisory board. Or, meet us in person: We will present the study and its outcomes at the INSPIRE conference 2016.

You have an INSPIRE model extension you'd like to see included in the inventory? Fork the repository on GitHub, add your page for the model, and send us a pull request. Let us know if you are not familiar with this approach and we'll help you to get it included.
