Advisory Board and Expert Group Meeting, 21st of April 2016, Ispra

We've invited the INSPIRE community to contribution to this project from the first date, and we've now done our first meeting with the expert group as well as JRC and DG Environment representatives from the advisory board. We had ambitious objectives for this meeting:

  • Ensure a common understanding of the goals of the project among stakeholders
  • Get high-quality constructive input on structure and methodological aspects of the study
  • Get contributions to build a resource that provides value to experts and the wider INSPIRE community

Meeting agenda and presentations:

  1. Context of the project (Jandirk Bulens)
  2. Meeting Introduction, Project Methodology and Implementation State (Thorsten Reitz)
  3. Expert Introductions (All)
    1. KLIC-WIN as extension on INSPIRE utilities (Paul Janssen)
    2. Debbie Wilson, Ordnance Survey
  4. Summary of Survey results and Model inventory (Thorsten Reitz)
  5. The ELF project - Modelling Guidelines (Morten Borrebaek)
  6. Design Studio: The INSPIRE Extension Methodology (All)
  7. Model Presentations
    1. INSPIRE extensions: With examples from SOSI and INSPIRE Planned Land Use (Morten Borrebaek)
    2. Air Quality Reporting based on INSPIRE (Katharina Schleidt)
    3. The GeoSmartCity Data model (Stefania Morrone)
  8. Identify and name patterns in example models (All)
