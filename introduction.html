Objectives and Context

INSPIRE, the European Directive establishing an Infrastructure for Spatial Information in the European Community, provides a large number of data models. While these cover many core use cases, they often need to be extended to serve specific organisational needs.

Geonovum has commissioned wetransform to research and document patterns that are emerging for such model extensions. Together with the INSPIRE MIGs and other expert groups, we aim to answer the following questions:

  • How to extend the INSPIRE data models in an uniform and standardised way?
  • Are we able - based on the currently best practices in Europe – to develop a standardised approach with patterns to extend the INSPIRE data models?

As a first step towards answering these questions, we have prepared a survey. The questionnaire linked blow is about experiences with data modelling and the possibilities to develop INSPIRE extensions. And of course to collect all best practises in order to study and develop uniform extensions patterns that already exist.

Methodology

We apply agile and lean principles to standards development:

  1. Iterate quickly
  2. Integrate and deploy early
  3. Drive measurable improvement with real data

For specifications such as INSPIRE this means we analyse existing usage of the standard, measure the performance of implementations and then modify the specification. We test these modifications by transforming data and by publishing services using the new design, e.g. a model that extends an INSPIRE data specification.

This approach is based on the harmonisation methodology developed in the HUMBOLDT project and then refined in more than 20 other projects afterwards. We use different tools for modeling, transformation and publishing, such as Enterprise Architect, GeoServer, deegree and Shapechange. In particular, the model driven, data augmented design process uses hale»studio (HUMBOLDT Alignment Editor) 2.9.4+ and hale»connect 1.0.

Our project management methodology is based on agile foundations. For a project focused on services such as data harmonization consulting like this one, we use a Kanban approach. Open tasks are documented in our issue management software, and all project participants are provided access to the current tasks and status information.

We assure the quality of deliverables through continuous communication with the stakeholders, through internal reviews of documents and through continuous testing of software and transformation projects. We manage generic project risks actively through documentation, involvement of additional staff and clear communication of expectations with project stakeholders. Specific risks for a harmonization project such as problematic source data quality are identified in the first phase of the project, and appropriate mitigation strategies are identified with project stakeholders.

The overall project quality will furthermore be ensured by an advisory committee. The advisory committee (AC) consists of representatives of representatives of DG ENV, EEA and JRC, as well as of Geonovum and wetransform. The responsibility of the AC is to monitor the progress of the project periodically (for every milestone) and to provide feedback on deliverables. They will also help to provide access to the INSPIRE community through channels such as the MIG-T, MIG-P, LMO and SDICs. The AC is expected to meet virtually once per month.

Contributions

This website is hosted as a Github page on a public Github repository. This means it is easy for anyone to suggest changes and additions. It is even possible to edit the page completely online, without special tools or the need to download the entire page. The principal process for making a contribution is as follows:

  1. Log in to Github.
  2. Visit the repository where the content of this study is hosted and switch to the gh-pages branch.
  3. Create a Fork of the inspire-extensions repository.
  4. In your forked repository, perform edits, either online by using the online Markdown and HTML editors, or offline by cloning the repository to your computer
  5. When you have completed your edit or addition, save/commit your changes, and create a Pull Request to let us know you want your changes to be included.
  6. Get notified when we review and merge your request in, and see your contribution become visible here!
